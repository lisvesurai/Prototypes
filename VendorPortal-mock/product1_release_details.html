For your software release, you will need to indicate any export controls related to it or its content. Export Control laws require that products containing or calling encryption be properly authorized for export. Failure to comply could result in severe penalties.

Does your application use any encryption and/or information security functions directly or indirectly performed by your application? This includes any encryption within the application on the BlackBerry (including HTTPS) AND/OR any encrypted connection the application makes to an external system.

Does your Application use or access encryption from the BlackBerry operating system in order to perform any cryptographic functionalities? (e.g. to enable openssl/libssl; Libcurl/ libcrypto; QNXCrypto; Certicom)

Does your Application use or access encryption from a source other than that found in the BlackBerry operating system? (e.g. Bouncy Castle; Android Libraries)

Has your application received an import and supply permit in France? You will need to provide the details of the French import classification received by your application from the French Government.

Please attach a copy in PDF format of the Permit that confirms the import and supply into France for your application.

Please provide the Export Classification Control Number applicable to your application. This answer is mandatory if you are a US Vendor or your application contains US technology.

Does your application support user generated content?

If your application supports user generated content such as images, text, profile names and messages, you will need to confirm that appropriate safeguard are in place to protect users - especially minors, and monitor and manage inappropriate conduct.

You prominently disclose the existence and nature of the safeguards.

You conform to relevant business practices to the disclosure made about the safeguards.

You established processes to immediately (within 24 hours) acknowledge receipt of a complaint about inappropriate conduct or activity and promptly (within three (3) business days) provide an explanation to the complainant of the steps taken to address the complaint.
